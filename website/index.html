Objective-Aware Sequence Modelling for Drift Forecasting and Anomaly Detection in Quantum Hardware

We reformulate superconducting-qubit reliability as a multivariate, mean-reverting forecasting-and-anomaly-detection problem and evaluate four sequence-model families under a single leakage-free protocol. Coherence is demonstrably forecastable, and the optimal detector is objective-dependent: a universally dominant architecture does not exist. The complete manuscript is provided below.

Background & Motivation

Why qubit-parameter drift is a sequence-modelling problem

Superconducting processors are not static devices. The relaxation time $T_1$, the dephasing time $T_2$, gate fidelities, readout-assignment error, error-per-Clifford and tunable-coupling parameters all evolve as functions of temperature, flux noise, two-level-system dynamics and recalibration history. Within the NISQ regime this bounds the usable circuit depth; within the fault-tolerant regime it threatens the assumption that physical error rates remain below threshold for sufficiently long that error correction suppresses logical errors.

At the scale of hundreds to thousands of qubits, determining when a device has drifted out of specification and which intervals warrant recalibration constitutes a data problem that exceeds manual inspection. The calibration stream is a multivariate stochastic process exhibiting slow periodic structure, secular degradation and stochastic fluctuation; forecasting it and flagging off-manifold windows maps onto modern time-series learning. The gap is the absence of decision-oriented guidance on which model family to deploy, at what parameter cost, and for which objective.

Contributions & Novelty

Precisely what is novel

The manuscript advances three contributions; each is reproducible end-to-end on commodity CPU hardware.

  1. A leakage-free, objective-aware benchmark. We formalise quantum-hardware reliability as a joint forecasting-and-anomaly-detection problem over a physically motivated, reproducible telemetry generator, and release a single leakage-free benchmark that evaluates four sequence-model families on this task and on three heterogeneous real-world regimes.
  2. Model selection must be objective-aware. The GRU is a robust, parameter-efficient generalist — the sole model to attain non-trivial incident detection — whereas the Transformer is a specialist that excels on periodic calibration-like signals yet generalises poorly. Architectures trade off across settings.
  3. A reconstruction detector with a provable control parameter. We introduce a low-rank reconstruction-based drift detector and identify the bottleneck rank as the control parameter that separates drift from nominal behaviour — an interpretable, training-free-at-inference monitor supported by an empirical-optimality theorem.
Mathematics & Method

The model, the estimators, and why they hold

Two theorems account for the two headline empirical findings: why forecast skill increases with horizon, and why detection is strongest at low reconstruction rank.

Drift generator (physics)

A mean-reverting multivariate telemetry process

For qubit $q$ at time $t$ (hours), the relaxation time superposes a 48-hour recalibration-like oscillation, secular ageing, and Gaussian fluctuation:

$$ T_1(q,t) = \max\!\Big(10,\; 80 + 20\sin\!\big(\tfrac{2\pi t}{48} + 0.8q\big) - 0.05\,t + \varepsilon\Big), \qquad \varepsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 2^2). $$

$T_2 = 0.9\,T_1\,\eta$ tracks $T_1$; the remaining channels are analogous bounded processes. A window is out-of-specification when $T_1 < 50~\mu\text{s}$ or single-qubit fidelity $< 0.998$. The configuration comprises $Q=5$ qubits, $N=200$ half-hour steps (100 h), and seven channels.

Leak-free windowing

Sliding windows, chronological splits

Series are segmented into windows of length $L=32$ predicting an $H=8$-step $T_1$ horizon, pooled across qubits. Leakage is precluded by chronological splitting: $70/15/15$ for the sequence-model benchmark, per-qubit $80/20$ for the forecasting baselines, and randomised $70/30$ for the detector (admissible only because it is fit on nominal windows exclusively). Scalers are fit on training data and applied forward.

Ridge closed form

Multivariate ridge forecaster

With flattened windows as the rows of $X$ and 8-step horizons as the rows of $Y$, ridge regression solves $\min_W \lVert XW - Y\rVert_2^2 + \alpha\lVert W\rVert_2^2$ with the closed form ($\alpha=5$)

$$ W^\star = (X^\top X + \alpha I)^{-1} X^\top Y. $$

The $\alpha I$ term renders the normal equations invertible despite correlated channels. The baselines are persistence, climatology, and AR-ridge (the 32-step $T_1$ history alone).

Horizon-skill theorem

Why skill widens with horizon

For a weakly stationary, zero-mean process with variance $\gamma_0$ and autocorrelation $\rho(h)$, persistence has risk $2\gamma_0(1-\rho(h))$ and the optimal single-lag predictor risk $\gamma_0(1-\rho(h)^2)$, so

$$ S(h) = 1 - \frac{R_\star(h)}{R_{\mathrm{pers}}(h)} = \frac{1-\rho(h)}{2}. $$

As $\rho(h)$ decays, $S(h)$ increases monotonically toward $\tfrac12$. This bound is predicated on the most recent sample alone; the multivariate ridge surpasses it by exploiting the full 32-step, 7-channel history — measured skill attains 72% at horizon 8.

PCA reconstruction detector

Drift as off-manifold residual energy

We fit the top-$k$ right singular vectors $V_k$ of the centred nominal matrix and project with $P_k = V_kV_k^\top$. The drift score $s_k(x) = \lVert x - xP_k\rVert_2^2$ is, by an empirical-optimality theorem (Rayleigh–Ritz), the smallest residual attainable by any rank-$k$ linear monitor fitted on nominal data. When $k = d$, $P_k = I$, the score is constant and detection collapses to chance — a theorem, not a tuning artefact.

ROC-AUC (Mann–Whitney)

The scale-free yardstick for detection

For independent drift $X^{+}$ and nominal $X^{-}$ windows, $\mathrm{AUC} = \Pr[s(X^{+}) > s(X^{-})] + \tfrac12\Pr[s(X^{+}) = s(X^{-})]$. AUC is invariant under any strictly increasing reparametrisation of $s$ — including per-feature affine rescaling — which is why it, rather than MAE/RMSE, serves as the basis for the cross-domain comparison.

Experimental Design & Statistics

How the numbers were produced

The benchmark separates replicated quantities from single-run point estimates and reports the metric matched to each monitoring objective.

Replication & uncertainty

What ± means here

Forecasting metrics are aggregated over n = 5 generator seeds, and the reconstruction detector over n = 8 randomised splits. Uncertainties are mean ± standard deviation. The deep sequence-model metrics (the thermal & cross-domain tables) are single deterministic, seeded runs reported as point estimates — stated explicitly, and not as confidence intervals.

Leakage control

Train/test windowing

Chronological splits exclude future intervals from training: $70/15/15$ for the sequence-model benchmark, per-qubit $80/20$ for the baselines, and randomised $70/30$ for the detector (fit on nominal windows exclusively). The window length is 32, the horizon is 8, pooled across five qubits.

Model families compared

Four sequence models + baselines

A single-layer Elman RNN, a two-layer LSTM, a two-layer GRU, and an encoder-only Transformer (pre-LN, $d_\text{model}=128$, 4 heads, 3 layers). Each emits an 8-step forecast and a drift logit, trained with AdamW under a cosine schedule on $\mathrm{MSE}+\mathrm{BCE}$. The forecasting baselines are persistence, climatology, AR-ridge, and multivariate ridge.

ROC-AUC methodology

Threshold-free ranking

Detection is scored by F1 at the default threshold and by threshold-free ROC-AUC (Mann–Whitney $U$). Both are reported because they diverge: a model can rank well (high AUC) yet detect nothing at a fixed operating point (zero F1). The cross-domain evaluation employs three NAB datasets as analogues for periodic, failure-bearing and irregular regimes.

Unsupervised drift detector

Low rank separates drift

Rank-3 reconstruction separates regimes with ROC-AUC 0.72 on a held-out split (0.70 ± 0.05 over 8 randomised splits). Sweeping the bottleneck rank, detection is strongest at low rank (0.87 ± 0.02 at rank 1) and collapses toward chance as the code becomes over-complete and memorises drifted windows.

Drift resides off a low-rank manifold; the rank is the false-alarm control parameter.
Key statistical comparison

F1 and ROC-AUC disagree by design

A model can rank intervals well (high ROC-AUC) yet detect nothing at a fixed threshold (zero F1) — as evidenced in the thermal table, where only the GRU satisfies both criteria. We report both and treat threshold selection as an integral part of the monitoring problem.

The reported metric is matched to the objective, not selected post hoc.

Conclusion & Impact

Match the architecture to the monitoring objective

This work reframes reliability monitoring as a tractable, decision-oriented machine-learning problem with an actionable rule: deploy the GRU as a default generalist and incident detector, and reserve the Transformer for periodic signals. The failure of strong models outside their domain of specialisation cautions against single-metric benchmarking; the low-rank manifold result furnishes operators with a single interpretable control parameter.

Limitations (stated candidly, from the paper)

  • The telemetry generator is physically motivated but constitutes a model, not measured device data; cross-domain validation employs standard NAB benchmarks as analogues, not quantum-calibration logs.
  • The absolute detection scores are modest, as anticipated for a rigorous, untuned benchmark — we refrain from any state-of-the-art claim.
  • The deep sequence-model metrics are single seeded runs (point estimates), not multi-seed confidence intervals.

Outlook

  • Validation on archival calibration records from operating processors.
  • Uncertainty-aware forecasting via Monte-Carlo dropout or deep ensembles.
  • Coupling the forecaster to a recalibration policy under an explicit cost model.

Open, hardware-agnostic and rapid to reproduce — a leakage-controlled, CPU-reproducible substrate for quantum-processor reliability research.
