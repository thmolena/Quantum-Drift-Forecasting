Quantum Drift Forecasting

Time-Series and Transformer-Based Modeling of Quantum Hardware Behavior, Calibration Drift, and Noise Analysis

Superconducting quantum processors undergo continuous decoherence-time (T1, T2) drift and gate-fidelity degradation between calibration cycles. This project trains recurrent and Transformer architectures on multi-qubit telemetry to forecast calibration metrics, detect anomalous drift, and schedule proactive recalibration.

Model Architectures

RNN

VanillaRNN

Baseline Elman RNN. Short-range dependencies. ~18 K parameters.

LSTM

LSTMForecaster

2-layer stacked LSTM, dropout 0.2. ~133 K parameters.

GRU

GRUForecaster

2-layer GRU, fewer params than LSTM with comparable accuracy. ~100 K.

TF

TransformerForecaster

Pre-LN encoder, d=128, 4 heads, 3 layers. ~265 K parameters.

AE

AnomalyDetector

Encoder-decoder autoencoder trained unsupervised on stable windows.

Research Summary

01

Data Acquisition

5 qubits × 200 time steps at 0.5 h resolution. 7 calibration features per step.

02

Sequence Modeling

Sliding windows (seq_len=48, horizon=8). Temporal 80/20 train-test split.

03

Training

AdamW + CosineAnnealing. Combined loss α·MSE + (1−α)·BCE.

04

Deployment

Flask REST API, MC-Dropout + conformal intervals, ~30% fewer wasted calibration cycles.
