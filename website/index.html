Executive Summary

Central empirical findings

The benchmark supports two principal claims. The GRU is the leading recurrent architecture for incident-aware thermal monitoring, while the Transformer is the leading model for anomaly ranking on periodic calibration-like telemetry. The website therefore presents the study as an objective-aware benchmark rather than as a claim of universal superiority for one model family.

Better performance in this project means stronger detection of meaningful failure windows, stronger prioritization of anomalous intervals for review, and lower average forecasting error when sequential operational signals are monitored over time.

Recurrent Benchmark

GRU leads incident-aware monitoring

In the thermal benchmark, the GRU is the strongest recurrent result because it combines incident detection, perfect recall, strong anomaly ranking, and a lower parameter count than the higher-capacity recurrent alternative.

Operational reading: stronger early-warning support for failure escalation in monitored hardware systems.
Calibration Ranking

Transformer leads anomaly triage

In the periodic telemetry regime, the Transformer delivers the strongest ranking result, making it the most useful option when suspicious intervals must be sorted before human review or recalibration activity.

Operational reading: more efficient review of suspect operating periods.
Combined Benchmark

GRU leads average forecasting quality

Across the full benchmark, the GRU records the strongest average MAE, RMSE, and ROC-AUC profile among the compared models, indicating that its gains extend across multiple time-series regimes rather than a single isolated case.

Operational reading: steadier performance when one monitoring pipeline spans multiple data sources.
Operational Meaning

What better performance means in real use

The reported improvements matter because they map directly to the repository’s monitoring tasks.

  • In thermal-failure monitoring, stronger GRU detection improves the probability that meaningful fault windows are surfaced in time for intervention.
  • In calibration-oriented anomaly review, stronger Transformer ranking improves how engineering attention is allocated across suspicious intervals.
  • In cross-dataset forecasting, stronger GRU averages improve monitoring stability when several operational time-series streams are analyzed in one workflow.
